    VEGETABLE PATTIES
    Ingredıents:
    • 1 cup fine bulgur
    • 1 cup boiling water
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 cup finely grated mixed vegetables (carrots, zucchini, bell peppers, onions)
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    • Fresh parsley, finely chopped (for garnish)
    Instructions:
    1. Place the fine bulgur in a large bowl.
    2. Pour the boiling water over the bulgur, cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap, and let it sit for 15-20 minutes until the bulgur absorbs the water and becomes soft.
    3. Once the bulgur is ready, add the tomato paste, pepper paste (if using), grated mixed vegetables, olive oil, salt, cumin, paprika, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using).
    4. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
    5. Knead the mixture with your hands for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes a soft and homogenous dough-like consistency.
    6. Take a portion of the mixture and shape it into small, round or oval patties with your hands.
    7. Optionally, create a hollow in the center of each patty to add a filling later.
    8. Place the vegetable patties on a serving platter. Garnish with finely chopped fresh parsley.
    9. If desired, fill the hollow in the center of each patty with lettuce leaves, cherry tomatoes, and cucumber slices.
    10. Serve the Vegtable Patties as a delicious and healthy appetizer or main dish.

    KAYGANA
    Ingredıents:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup lukewarm water
    • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • Olive oil for frying
    Instructions:
    1. In a small bowl, combine the lukewarm water, active dry yeast, and sugar. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy
    2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
    3. Pour the frothy yeast mixture into the flour and mix to form a sticky dough.
    4. Flour a clean surface and transfer the dough onto it
    5. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
    6. Place the dough back into the mixing bowl, cover it with a kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size
    7. Once the dough has risen, divide it into small portions (about the size of a golf ball)
    8. Take one portion of the dough and flatten it with your hands to form a round or oval shape
    9. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a bit of olive oil.
    10. Place the shaped dough into the pan and cook until both sides are golden brown. This usually takes 2-3 minutes per side
    11. Repeat the process for the remaining portions of dough, adding more oil to the pan as needed.
    12. Serve the kaygana warm. It can be enjoyed on its own, with cheese, or with a drizzle of honey.

    CHEESE STUFFED SULTAN PEAS
    Ingredıents:
    • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas
    • Water for boiling
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
    • 1/4 cup parsley, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Instructions:
    1. Bring a pot of water to boil. Add the peas and salt, and cook for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender. Drain and set aside.
    2. In a bowl, combine crumbled feta cheese, finely chopped parsley, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well to form a creamy filling.
    3. Once the peas have cooled slightly, carefully scoop out a small portion from each pea to create a cavity.
    4. Fill each pea with the cheese filling mixture.
    5. In a saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onion and minced garlic, sauté until softened
    6. Add crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 10 minutes until the sauce thickens
    7. Arrange the stuffed peas on a serving platter. Pour the warm tomato sauce over the peas.
    8. Garnish with additional chopped parsley or a drizzle of olive oil if desired.
    9. Serve the Peynirli Sultani Bezelye warm as a delicious appetizer or side dish.

    KIDNEY BEAN DISH
    Ingredıents:
    • 1 cup beans , soaked overnigh
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, diced
    • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 3 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • Salt and black pepper to taste
    • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
    Instructions:
    1. Rinse the dry borlotti beans thoroughly and soak them in water overnight. Drain before using.
    2. In a large pot, add the soaked borlotti beans and cover with water. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook until the beans are tender but not mushy (usually about 45-60 minutes). Drain the cooked beans.
    3. In a separate pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
    4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
    5. Add minced garlic, diced tomatoes, and chopped green bell pepper. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
    6. Stir in tomato paste, red pepper flakes (if using), ground cumin, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the flavors meld.
    7. Add the cooked borlotti beans to the vegetable mixture. Stir well to combine and let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes.
    8. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley.
    9. Serve the Kidney Beans Dish hot, optionally with a slice of lemon on the side.
